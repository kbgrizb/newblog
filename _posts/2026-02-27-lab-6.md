I turned this online grocery store scenario into a data modeling framework with the entities "item,"
"customer," and "selectedItem," as well as the relationship "order" so that customers can order groceries
based on the items available. I struggled a little bit to make the relationship diagram and figure out
the cardinalities between the entities, but I landed on diagrams that I think make sense. For my user
stories, I was a little bit confused about how to denote size. I opted for using a scale from 1-10, where
10 would take the most time, and 1 would take the least.

User Stories:

1. As a customer, I want to buy groceries so that I can eat.
(size: 10, connected to all of the stories below)
2. As a customer, I want to enter my address so that I can recieve deliveries.
(size: 2, connected to stories 1 and 4)
3. As a customer, I want to see all available groceries to that I can choose what I need.
(size: 6, connected to story 1)
4. As a customer, I want to choose pickup or delivery so that I can get my groceries how I want.
(size: 3, connected to stories 2 and 5)
5. As a customer, I want to choose a time for my order to be fulfilled so that it gets to me when I need it.
(size: 5, connected to story 4)

out how the customers are connected to the items they select.
